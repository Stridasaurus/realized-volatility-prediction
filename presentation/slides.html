Can a Small LSTM Beat HAR?

Honest measurement of a deep-learning edge in daily, range-based volatility forecasting — and where that edge does, and doesn't, come from.

Strider Settgast2026‑07‑08
The Setup

HAR's reputation was earned on data we don't have

  • HAR (Corsi, 2009): three OLS terms on daily / weekly / monthly lagged volatility — famously hard to beat.
  • That reputation was earned on high-frequency realized variance.
  • This project has only daily OHLC — so the targets are daily range-based variance proxies (Rogers–Satchell + overnight; Garman–Klass), a distinct estimator family.
  • The real question: does HAR's dominance transfer to this regime — and can anything beat it here?
Method — one slide

A protocol built to make self-deception hard

  • Frozen snapshot — SPY + VIX, SHA-256 checksummed, pinned end date 2026-06-30.
  • Chronological walk-forward — train 2005–17, val 2018–19, test 2020–2026, monthly refit (78 folds).
  • Hyperparameters tuned once (50-trial Optuna) and frozen before any test-region contact.
  • 10-seed ensembles, Diebold–Mariano (HLN-corrected) inference — edge requires p<0.05 and ≥80% seed agreement.

Where it comes from

Features, not architecture

  • RV+aux beats RV-only head-to-head (TV h=1 p = 7.2×10⁻⁹) — the edge traces to the auxiliary features, not the sequence model.
  • Present at both horizons — nearly the same relative size at h=5 despite reused, unre-tuned hyperparameters.
  • Present on both targets — unchanged whether overnight variance is included (TV) or excluded (OC).
  • Present in every regime — COVID, calm 2021, the 2022 bear, and the 2023–26 recovery. The cumulative edge never gives any of it back.

A caveat worth knowing

The network is data-hungry

Fig. 3 — QLIKE vs. training-window length (RV-only, TV h=1)
  • At ~2 years of history, HAR wins decisively (p = 7.6×10⁻⁷).
  • At ~5 years, HAR still leads (p = 0.084).
  • Only at the full ~12.6-year frozen window does the network reach parity with HAR.
  • Practical reading: don't expect this edge on short histories.
Limitations

What this result doesn't claim

  • Multiplicity — many DM tests are reported; all of them together, and the headline p-values sit far below any reasonable correction.
  • Statistical, not economic — no transaction-cost or vol-targeting backtest; one asset (SPY) only.
  • Proxy-scoped, floor not ceiling — TV claims speak to total daily variance, OC to the trading session only; this is a lower bound on extractable signal, not a ceiling.
Conclusion
The floor claim

There is economically meaningful predictive information beyond HAR — at least 17% of QLIKE — and it lives in the auxiliary market-state features, not the deep architecture.
